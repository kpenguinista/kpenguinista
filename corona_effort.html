1. Introduction¶

During the past 4 months of quarantine, our news feeds have been flooded with daily media coverage of the coronavirus pandemic and its statistics. As we progress through the summer months, there has been an increasing interest in what the road to recovery looks like in the months ahead and in the measures that have been adopted thus far. We hear of new policies being implemented everyday during these "unprecedented times," as many have phrased to describe the crisis. In some countries, there has been significant reductions in cases due to well-adopted measurements. On the other hand, as the cases in the US continues to rise, the nation is on track as the epicenter of the pandemic, and there is an abundance of news outlets discussing the relatively minimal efforts and slow progress made by the US government.

Since there are plenty of public data sets for the pandemic, there are several questions I find to be worth exploring. I will begin with the question most relevant to the current days of the pandemic: Is the number of cases declining during the summer months? How has the death rate changed globally? by continent? Then, I will move onto analyzing effort; that is, exploring the question, how much effort have countries put in as a countermeasure against the spread of the pandemic?

For analyzing effort, I want to create a self-defined measure of effort because the available data sets mostly concern the effects of the coronavirus compared to the efforts. For instance, the Bureau of Labor Statistics measured effects using the current population survey. I will utilize this self-defined measurement to visualize and thereby view the relative efforts made by countries through quantitative lens.

At the end, I hope to have provided insight to the public of the global trends of the coronavirus pandemic.

4. Is the number of cases declining during the summer?¶

There have been many individuals speculating and hopeful for the slowing of the virus' spread during the summer season. However, health officials and researchers have expressed doubt on a decline. Notably, the NIH director Dr. Francis Collins has released a statement about the health community's doubt of seasonal decline of the coronavirus. First, I will analyze the number of cases beginning in the spring. Then, since we are now a month into the summer season, I will analyze the trend of coronavirus cases starting from June 21, 2020, which is the first day of summer. Last of all, I will compare the summer rate thus far to the spring trend.

6. Calculating Effort¶

With thes raw numbers and dates from the data frame, I want to measure governmental effort during the pandemic, which is going to be categorized into two types of effort: the policy enforcement effort and use of resources effort. Both efforts are measured as a ranking, or in other words, effort that is based on relative performance.

6.1 Policy Enforcement Effort¶

Policy enforcement effort will be calculated through ranking based on policy effectiveness. I am going to do so based on the linear trend in increase per day of the population percentages of cases and deaths for each country following the date of their first policy implementation. We cannot rank solely by the case and death counts because the raw numbers themselves do not provide the context, for there is a directly proportional relationship between raw quantity and population size. Hence, it is the percentages that provide insight.

In [24]:
# Filter out SettingWithCopyWarning that appears when summing data frame columns
warnings.filterwarnings('ignore')

df['cases_and_deaths_pct'] = ((df['total_cases'] + df['total_deaths']) / df['population']) * 100
post_data = df[(not df.date is np.nan) & (df.date >= df.first_policy_date)]

policy_effort = pd.DataFrame(post_data.iso_code.unique(), columns=['iso_code'])
policy_effort['trends'] = np.float64(0) # placeholder

# Dates were transformed into epoch time because trend can only be found between scalar values
for c, v in policy_effort.iterrows():
    c_df = post_data[post_data.iso_code == v.iso_code]
    c_df['date_epoch'] = c_df['date'].apply(lambda x: datetime.timestamp(x))
    m, b = np.polyfit(c_df.date_epoch, c_df.cases_and_deaths_pct, 1)
    policy_effort.at[c, 'trends'] = m

policy_effort['ranking'] = policy_effort.trends.rank()  

# Order by rank 
policy_effort = policy_effort.sort_values('ranking')

6.2 Use of Resources Effort¶

While policy effectiveness is a significant aspect of effort, it is not enough to cover the whole context needed to be considered to yield accurate observations. Countries have differing economic standings and these make a critical impact on the options and limitations they have in order to take measures against the coronavirus. To rank based on how well did a country make use of their resources at hand, I am going to utilize the stringency index and GDP per capita. The deviation from the linear trend of stringency indes versus GDP per capita will be ranked for calculating relative use of resources.
